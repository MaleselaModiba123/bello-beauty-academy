## Analysis

The fork count of 27 exceeding the star count of 25 is a positive signal. In open-source projects, forks represent a stronger form of engagement than stars because they indicate that a peer wanted to explore or build on the code locally rather than simply bookmarking the project.

Five out of six comments were positive and focused on specific aspects of the repository: the README structure, the Swagger UI, the C4 architecture diagrams, the CONTRIBUTING.md, and the CI/CD pipeline. The one critical comment from Reviewer 6 about the non-standard folder structure was the most actionable piece of feedback and directly led to an update in CONTRIBUTING.md that explicitly explains the project structure and why it differs from a standard Spring Boot layout.

The absence of pull requests is an honest reflection of the peer review timeline. A full reflection on the peer engagement experience is available in [REFLECTION.md](./REFLECTION.md).
